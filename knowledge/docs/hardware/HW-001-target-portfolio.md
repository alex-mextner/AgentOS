# Hardware Target Portfolio

## Tracks

### QEMU baseline

Deterministic AArch64 platform for kernel semantics, continuous integration, fault injection, replay, and architecture-independent conformance tests.

### Documented and open boards

Physical targets selected by documentation quality, recoverability, serial/debug access, upstreamable interfaces, firmware redistribution terms, supply continuity, and community activity. These targets prove clean native portability without Android or Linux ABI dependencies.

### Open phone-form-factor research

Phone-shaped devices with isolated modems and comparatively accessible hardware are used to study suspend, battery, telephony state machines, sensors, audio routing, and enclosure constraints. They are not assumed to meet flagship quality.

### Pixel 9 quality target

Pixel 9 establishes the camera, display, power, responsiveness, connectivity, and industrial-design quality ceiling. Android/Linux use is restricted to evidence, recovery, behavioural tracing, and temporary isolated bridges. Native feasibility proceeds through explicit boot, display, storage, power, GPU, radio, audio, camera, and security gates.

### Camera laboratory

Camera work is treated as a cross-device programme: RAW acquisition, sensor timing, ISP contracts, 3A, calibration, burst processing, temporal fusion, colour science, video, stabilization, power, and objective/subjective image-quality testing.

### Future custom device

Board packages, device-service IDL, secure boot, OTA, diagnostics, regulatory evidence, firmware licensing, and supply-chain records are designed so a future ODM/JDM device can replace research targets without rewriting product layers.

## Selection scorecard

Every candidate is scored for documentation, firmware availability, debug access, boot-chain control, GPU/display feasibility, camera feasibility, modem isolation, power management, security support, cost, availability, and legal constraints.
